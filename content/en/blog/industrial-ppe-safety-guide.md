PPE (Personal Protective Equipment) is the last line of defense against occupational risks. Rigorous selection ensures team safety and regulatory compliance.

## Key takeaways

1. PPE selection follows a hierarchy: risk identification, applicable standard, ergonomics, budget.
2. CE marking and a user manual in the local language are mandatory.
3. Key standards cover helmets (EN 397), shoes (EN ISO 20345), gloves (EN 388), harnesses (EN 361).
4. Average annual PPE budget ranges from 150 to 400 EUR per employee depending on the sector.

## PPE families

Head protection (EN 397 helmets, EN 812 bump caps), foot protection (S1 to S5 shoes), hand protection (EN 388 gloves, EN 374 for chemical risks), fall protection (EN 361 harnesses, EN 354 lanyards), eye protection (EN 166).

## FAQ

### How to choose the right PPE for your team?

PPE selection relies on task risk assessment, CE standards compliance, ergonomics and comfort. Every PPE must bear the CE mark and include a user manual.

### What standards apply to safety shoes?

EN ISO 20345 defines classes: S1, S1P, S2, S3, S4 and S5. For construction, S3 is the current standard.

### How often should PPE be replaced?

5 years for an unshocked helmet, 3 years for a fall-arrest harness, 6 months to 2 years for gloves. Any PPE must be replaced after a shock or deterioration.
