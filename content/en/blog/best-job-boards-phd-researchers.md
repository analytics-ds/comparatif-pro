> 2. AcademicPositions consolidates 20,000 open academic roles worldwide in English, covering assistant professorships, postdocs and research fellowships.
> 3. Nature Careers, Science Careers and HigherEdJobs dominate the scientific and higher education markets with over 25,000 combined postings.

## Global PhD job market overview in 2026

The global PhD job market has expanded significantly over the past decade, driven by rising industrial R&D spending, artificial intelligence research and the life sciences boom. The OECD counts over 2.5 million PhD holders across its member countries in 2026, with approximately 230,000 new doctoral graduates each year.

The **best job boards for PhDs** split between three categories: institutional portals for academic recruitment, specialized research platforms for postdocs and industry sites with PhD filters. Effective job search for a doctoral candidate combines multiple canals rather than relying on a single platform.

### A market split between academia and industry

The NSF Survey of Doctorate Recipients 2024 reveals a clear shift in US PhD employment. 65 percent now work outside academia, primarily in industry (45 percent), followed by government (12 percent) and non-profits (8 percent). The share has grown from 55 percent in 2010, reflecting both tight academic job markets and strong industrial demand for advanced expertise.

Employment outcomes remain globally strong. Approximately 94 percent of US PhDs are employed three years after graduation, with STEM PhDs reaching 97 percent according to the same NSF data. The PhD salary premium reaches 12 to 20 percent mid-career in industry compared to bachelor's degree peers in equivalent roles.

## Dedicated platforms for PhDs and doctoral candidates

Several platforms target doctoral profiles specifically, with qualified postings and tailored services. They form the core of the search strategy for research-oriented profiles.

### EURAXESS, the European reference portal

**EURAXESS** is the official European Commission portal for researcher mobility. It lists more than 25,000 active positions across all 40 member countries. Listings cover every career stage: doctoral candidate, postdoc, research associate, group leader and full professor.

The portal highlights Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA), which fund international postdocs for one to three years with a gross monthly salary ranging from 5,000 to 6,500 euros depending on host country. More than 11,000 MSCA fellowships are awarded annually under the Horizon Europe 2021-2027 framework programme.

### AcademicPositions and international academic sites

**AcademicPositions** lists approximately 20,000 open academic roles worldwide, primarily in Europe, North America and Australia. The English-only platform focuses on postdoc positions, lectureships, tenure-track assistant professorships and research fellowships. It publishes about 3,500 new openings per month.

**Jobs.ac.uk** dominates the UK higher education market with 10,000 active listings at any given time, covering UK universities, research councils and affiliated institutions. **HigherEdJobs.com** serves the US market with over 40,000 annual postings from 4,500 institutions. Similar platforms exist for regional markets like AcademicKeys, Inside Higher Ed Careers and Academic Jobs Online (mathematics and physics focus).

### Nature Careers and Science Careers

**Nature Careers** (operated by Springer Nature) is the flagship platform for natural sciences recruitment. It features 8,000 active postings with strong presence in biomedical research, chemistry, physics and environmental sciences. Employers include major universities, biotech companies and pharmaceutical firms.

**Science Careers**, operated by the American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS), complements Nature Careers with particular strength in the United States. It lists around 5,000 active positions and publishes quarterly salary surveys that constitute a reference for the scientific community.

## Institutional portals and research councils

Major public research organizations publish their openings on dedicated portals. These concentrate permanent positions (researcher, lecturer) and temporary contracts (postdoc, visiting scholar).

### European research institutions

Each major European research organization has its own portal:

- **CNRS** (France): 300 annual researcher positions and 1,500 fixed-term scientific contracts
- **Max Planck Society** (Germany): 350 group leader openings per year plus thousands of postdoc positions
- **Helmholtz Association** (Germany): 500+ annual openings across 18 research centers
- **CERN** (Switzerland): 300 fellowship and research positions per year in physics and engineering
- **EMBL** (European Molecular Biology Laboratory): 200 positions in life sciences across five sites

For a broader view of research tools and resources, see our selection of the [best resources for a doctorate](/en/blog/best-doctoral-research-resources/).

### US federal and university portals

US institutions operate individual careers pages plus consolidated platforms. The NIH (National Institutes of Health) lists 2,000 annual openings in biomedical research. NSF-funded institutions post through the NSF Faculty Jobs portal. Major university systems like UC Berkeley, Harvard, MIT and Stanford maintain dedicated doctoral and postdoc portals with thousands of annual openings.

### International industry-research platforms

Some platforms bridge academia and industry for PhD profiles. **ResearchGate Jobs** leverages the 20 million members of the academic social network to match PhDs with industry R&D openings. **HiredEducation** specializes in university administration roles. **Academia.edu Careers** complements the picture for humanities and social sciences PhDs.

## Generalist platforms adapted for PhDs

Major generalist job boards have developed features and filters for PhD candidates targeting industry roles. They remain essential for PhDs moving into the private sector.

### LinkedIn, Indeed and industry platforms

**LinkedIn** allows filtering by education level "Doctorate or equivalent" and by research-related keywords (R&D, research scientist, principal investigator). The platform counts around 3 million PhDs globally and is the preferred channel for industry sourcing. 70 percent of industry R&D recruiters actively source through LinkedIn according to a JobAtlas 2024 study.

**Indeed**, with over 250 million monthly visitors, serves as a volume aggregator with PhD filters. For engineering and tech PhDs specifically, platforms like Dice, Hired and Wellfound (AngelList Talent) offer better qualified matches, as detailed in our comparison of [engineering job boards](/en/blog/best-engineering-job-boards/).

### Specialized staffing and executive search

Several staffing firms specialize in PhD placements. **Kelly Scientific Resources** handles 10,000 annual placements in biotech, pharma and chemistry. **Hays Life Sciences** covers Europe and Asia with a strong presence in the UK, Germany and Switzerland. **SciTech Patent Art** specializes in scientific intellectual property roles requiring PhD expertise. **Korn Ferry** and **Russell Reynolds Associates** handle executive search for senior scientific leadership roles.

## How PhDs can successfully transition to industry

Transition from academia to industry remains a major challenge for PhDs worldwide. Studies consistently show that PhDs underestimate the transferable value of their doctoral work and struggle to reframe their skills in corporate vocabulary.

> "67 percent of industry recruiters report limited visibility on the actual skills of PhD candidates, even though 78 percent actively recruit doctoral profiles for R&D and innovation roles."
> — Nature Careers Industry Survey, 2024

### Five-step method for PhD job search

1. Prepare two CV versions: a detailed academic CV and a concise two-page industry CV
2. Identify three to five target sectors and map innovative companies by sector
3. Register on EURAXESS, AcademicPositions, LinkedIn and one industry-specific platform with SEO-optimized keywords
4. Attend at least two networking events per year organized by PhD associations or sector conferences
5. Reframe transferable skills such as project management, scientific writing and communication, drawing on frameworks described in our [project management certifications comparison](/en/blog/project-management-training-certifications/)

## Growth sectors and in-demand roles for PhDs

The most active sectors for PhD recruitment in 2026 concentrate on innovation and R&D. Pharmaceuticals and biotech lead with 15,000 annual PhD hires in the US alone according to BIO Industry Report 2025. Information technology and artificial intelligence follow with 12,000 hires, driven by shortages in data science, machine learning and deep learning.

Energy (nuclear, hydrogen, photovoltaics) accounts for 5,000 annual PhD positions, with strong demand for expertise in materials science, electrochemistry and numerical simulation. Aerospace and defense maintain a steady rhythm of 4,000 hires per year, split between public research labs (NASA, ESA, ONERA, DLR) and major industrial players.

### Most sought-after roles for PhDs

Functions that most value a PhD in 2026 are:

- AI and machine learning researcher: median starting salary 145,000 dollars in US tech hubs
- Senior data scientist with PhD: 120,000 to 180,000 dollars based on experience
- R&D engineer in materials and chemistry: 95,000 to 125,000 dollars
- Scientific affairs director and expert consultant: 130,000 dollars in consulting
- Industrial laboratory leader: 140,000 to 180,000 dollars after five years

Mastering project management methodologies remains a strong lever for PhDs targeting leadership roles.
