Choosing professional office furniture directly impacts productivity, employee wellbeing and corporate image. Between ergonomics, aesthetics and budget, decision criteria are numerous.

## Key takeaways

1. Professional furniture is selected on four criteria: ergonomics, modularity, durability and total cost.
2. EN 527 and EN 1335 standards define minimum technical requirements.
3. The budget per complete workstation ranges from 800 to 2500 EUR excl. VAT.
4. Modularity is becoming a strategic criterion as work modes evolve (remote work, flex office).

## Essential selection criteria

Ergonomics is the primary criterion. An adjustable chair (height and depth), a sit-stand desk and suitable lighting reduce musculoskeletal disorders by 35% according to occupational safety studies.

Modularity allows the space to adapt to organizational changes. Bench desks, mobile acoustic partitions and wheeled storage units enable quick reconfigurations.

## FAQ

### How to choose professional office furniture?

Selecting professional office furniture relies on four criteria: ergonomics (EN 1335 compliance), modularity (adaptable to evolving uses), durability (minimum 5-year warranty) and total cost of ownership including delivery and assembly.

### What budget should I plan to equip a professional office?

Plan between 800 and 2500 EUR excl. VAT per complete workstation (desk, ergonomic chair, storage). Executive setups range from 3000 to 8000 EUR excl. VAT. Collaborative spaces require an additional budget of 500 to 1500 EUR per person hosted.

### What standards apply to office furniture?

Key standards include EN 527 for work tables, EN 1335 for office seating, EN 14073 for storage units and Directive 90/270/EEC for screen-based workstations ergonomics.
