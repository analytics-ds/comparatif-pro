Industrial measuring instruments drive quality control, regulatory compliance and installation reliability. Selecting the right equipment requires understanding accuracy classes and certifications.

## Key takeaways

1. Main criteria are measured quantity, accuracy, ruggedness and certification.
2. IEC 61010 defines safety standards for multimeters (CAT I to CAT IV categories).
3. Fluke, Keysight, Rohde and Schwarz dominate the professional segment.
4. A properly calibrated instrument must be verified annually by an accredited laboratory.

## Instrument families

Sensors (temperature, pressure, flow, level) collect physical data. Portable instruments (multimeters, clamp meters, infrared thermometers) support field diagnostics. Stationary analyzers (oscilloscopes, spectrum analyzers) equip test benches.

## FAQ

### How to choose industrial measuring instruments?

Choosing an industrial measuring instrument depends on the measured quantity, required accuracy, operating environment and budget. Favor ISO 17025 certified brands for legal metrology.

### What is the difference between professional and consumer multimeters?

A professional multimeter offers CAT III or CAT IV safety rating, base accuracy below 0.5%, True RMS and CAT certification compliant with IEC 61010. Consumer models are limited to CAT II with 1 to 2% accuracy.

### What is an accuracy class in metrology?

The accuracy class expresses the maximum uncertainty of an instrument as a percentage of full scale. A class 0.1 instrument is 10 times more accurate than a class 1 instrument.
