An ERP structures the entire management of an SME: accounting, sales, inventory, production, HR. Choosing it commits the company for 7 to 10 years and determines its ability to grow.

## Key points

1. Four criteria dominate the selection: functional coverage, total cost, compatibility, scalability.
2. The average budget for an SME ERP ranges from 15,000 to 80,000 euros for the initial deployment.
3. Odoo, Sage, Dynamics 365 Business Central and SAP Business One dominate the SME market.
4. A well-implemented ERP typically delivers a positive ROI within 18 to 36 months.

## Selection criteria

Functional coverage must match business processes (trading, industry, services). Integration with existing tools (CRM, CAD, e-commerce) avoids duplicate data entry. The pricing model (perpetual licence, SaaS subscription) directly impacts cash flow.

## FAQ

### How do you choose an ERP for an SME?

Choosing an SME ERP relies on functional coverage, total cost of ownership, compatibility with existing tools and scalability. Count 6 to 12 months for a full deployment.

### How much does an ERP cost for an SME?

The cost of an SME ERP ranges between 15,000 and 80,000 euros for initial deployment, plus 3,000 to 15,000 euros annual maintenance. SaaS solutions start at 25 euros per user per month.

### What are the best ERPs for SMEs in 2026?

The most adopted ERPs among SMEs in 2026 are Odoo, Sage 100, Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central and SAP Business One.
