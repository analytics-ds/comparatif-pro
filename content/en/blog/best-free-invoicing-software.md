Issuing compliant invoices without spending a euro has become reality for very small structures since the arrival of cloud freemium and free solutions. **Free invoicing software** has become the natural entry point for freelancers, micro-entrepreneurs and small businesses wanting to abandon Excel or Word without committing to a subscription. The remaining question is which tool to choose, since behind the "0 €" promise, functional scopes vary widely.

This comparison analyzes the seven most relevant solutions on the European market in 2026, their strengths, limitations and the profiles for which each is best suited.

## Why switch to a dedicated invoicing tool

Working with a spreadsheet or word processor exposes you to several legal and operational risks: chronological numbering not guaranteed, missing legal mentions, lack of secure archiving, fragile traceability in case of tax audit. Since the 2018 anti-fraud VAT law and the gradual extension of B2B electronic invoicing planned between 2026 and 2027, obligations have tightened for all VAT-liable companies.

A modern invoicing solution, even in free version, brings three immediate benefits:

- **Automated legal compliance**: sequential invoice numbering, mandatory mentions pre-filled, time-stamped archiving.
- **Administrative time savings**: quote converted to invoice in one click, customizable templates, sending by email with delivery receipt.
- **Commercial steering**: payment tracking, automatic reminders, accounting exports usable by your accountant.

Freelancers still juggling between manual PDFs and forgotten transfers lose on average two to four hours per month on their administrative position. For [self-employed professionals seeking a dedicated tool](/en/blog/best-free-self-employed-software/), switching to a specialized platform is profitable from the first invoice.

## Henrri: the European reference for unsubscribed quotes and invoices

Published by Sage since 2015, Henrri remains in 2026 the most-used free invoicing solution by craftsmen and freelancers in France and other European countries. Its positioning relies on an unusual model: features are funded by upselling other Sage software (accounting, payroll), without intrusive advertising or limits imposed on the user.

Its key strengths:

- Unlimited quotes, invoices, credit notes, purchase orders and delivery notes.
- Full template customization with logo, colors and general terms.
- Read-only banking sync to track receipts.
- NF525 compliance and Factur-X compatibility for standardized issuance.

Its main limitation is the absence of integrated accounting: for VAT declaration and balance sheet, you either switch to a paid Sage module or export data to a [dedicated accounting software for small business](/en/blog/best-accounting-software-small-business/). For most pure invoicing needs, this scope is largely sufficient.

## Wave and Zervant: simple alternatives for freelancers

**Wave** is the international reference for fully free invoicing software. Originally Canadian and now widely used worldwide, Wave covers invoicing, payment tracking and basic accounting at no cost, with revenue generated through optional payment processing fees. The interface is in English and appeals to international freelancers.

**Zervant**, a Finnish solution present on the European market for ten years, plays the minimalism card. Its free version caps at five active clients but allows an unlimited number of invoices, suitable for freelancers with restricted portfolios. The mobile app enables invoicing from a job site or external intervention, a feature absent from several free competitors.

Both solutions share the same approach: cover the essentials of invoicing without forcing premature upgrades. For starting structures, this is generally sufficient during the first twelve to eighteen months of activity.

## Pennylane and Tiime: free invoicing coupled with accounting ecosystem

Several platforms initially positioned on collaborative accounting have opened their invoicing module for free use. **Pennylane** has offered since 2024 a free version covering unlimited issuance of quotes and invoices, with reminder management and treasury dashboard. The free tier stops at accounting (entry, FEC, declarations) which remains paid.

**Tiime** operates on a similar model: free invoicing to attract the target, paid accounting to monetize. Its advantage lies in the ecosystem: if the small business later switches to a complete formula, the invoicing history and customer relationships are preserved without migration.

For profiles anticipating rapid growth or already working with an accounting firm using these platforms, starting with their free version avoids costly migration twelve months later. Conversely, for strictly invoicing use without growth projection, tools like Henrri or Wave remain more direct.

## Criteria for choosing your free invoicing software

Before settling on a solution, four questions deserve a clear answer:

**1. What invoice volume per month?**
Below five monthly invoices, any tool will do. Beyond twenty invoices, automated reminders and banking sync become decisive, pointing toward Henrri or Pennylane.

**2. Issuance in Factur-X format from now?**
The generalization of B2B electronic invoicing in France gradually requires the standardized format. Editors Henrri, Pennylane and Tiime already offer Factur-X in their free or freemium versions, avoiding later migration.

**3. Need for ancillary modules (POS, accounting, inventory management)?**
For commerce or sales activity, integrating a [POS software adapted to small business](/en/blog/best-free-pos-software/) or an [inventory management tool](/en/blog/best-inventory-management-software/) in parallel is often more relevant than an isolated invoicing solution.

**4. Accountant accompaniment?**
If the firm already uses Pennylane or Tiime, starting on the same platform smooths exchanges. Otherwise, standard CSV or FEC exports (Henrri, Wave) are compatible with all accounting software on the market, including [integrated payroll solutions](/en/blog/best-payroll-software/).

## Limits of free versions and tipping points to paid

Free versions all have natural boundaries. Most frequent in 2026:

- **Multi-user**: collaboration with multiple people is generally reserved for paid versions (starting at 9 to 19 € per month depending on editor).
- **Purchase management and expense reports**: little integrated in free versions, except at Pennylane.
- **Advanced reporting**: detailed dashboards, forecasts, multi-axis analytics remain a premium argument.
- **Priority support**: free version often gives access only to online documentation and chatbot, not a human advisor.

The typical tipping point toward a paid formula sits around the twentieth to thirtieth monthly invoice, when the small business hires its first employee or opens a second professional bank account. At this stage, the cost of a paid subscription (10 to 30 € per month) is largely offset by productivity gains.
