A well-deployed CMMS reduces maintenance costs by 10 to 25% and improves industrial equipment availability. The choice balances functional coverage, costs and IS integration.

## Key takeaways

1. CMMS centralizes planning, interventions, inventory and maintenance indicators.
2. Typical gains are a 15 to 40% reduction in unplanned downtime.
3. Coswin, Altair, IBM Maximo, SAP PM and Mobility Work cover all segments.
4. Average budget: 15,000 to 50,000 EUR for SMBs, higher for large groups.

## Selection criteria

Functional coverage (preventive, corrective, predictive maintenance) must match business stakes. Integration with ERP and IoT sensors is becoming a key criterion. Mobility (field tablets) and ergonomics drive technician adoption.

## FAQ

### What is a CMMS and what is it used for?

CMMS centralizes industrial maintenance: preventive scheduling, work orders, spare parts inventory, failure history. It reduces unplanned downtime by 15 to 40%.

### Which CMMS to choose for an industrial SMB?

Coswin 8i and Altair are accessible French benchmarks. Mobility Work offers a SaaS model at 35 EUR per user. For large groups, IBM Maximo and SAP PM remain international standards.

### How much does CMMS deployment cost?

CMMS costs vary from 15,000 to 50,000 EUR for an SMB, up to several hundred thousand EUR for a large group.
