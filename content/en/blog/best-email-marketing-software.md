Email remains in 2026 the marketing channel with the best return on investment: 36 € generated per 1 € invested according to the latest Litmus study. **Email marketing software** has become an essential marketing productivity tool for small businesses, SMEs and e-commerce companies wanting to communicate effectively with their contacts.

This comparison analyzes the seven most relevant platforms on the market in 2026 according to audience size, automation level and business profile.

## Why a dedicated email marketing platform

Sending marketing emails from a personal mailbox (Gmail, Outlook) or a standard SMTP server exposes you to several problems in 2026: daily volume limit, poor deliverability, no tracking, GDPR non-compliance, risk of domain blacklisting. Beyond a few dozen recipients, using a professional platform becomes essential.

A modern email marketing solution brings three technical benefits:

- **Optimized deliverability**: automatic configuration of DNS records (SPF, DKIM, DMARC), IP reputation management, adaptation to mailbox provider requirements.
- **Integrated GDPR compliance**: consent management, one-click unsubscribe, opt-in traceability, contact removal on request.
- **Steering and optimization**: open rates, clicks, conversions, A/B test on subject and content, behavioral analysis.

Integration with commercial tools is also a key criterion. The complementarity between the sending system and the [CRM software](/en/blog/best-crm-software/) determines the quality of segmentation and automated sequences.

## Brevo: the European all-in-one reference

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue until 2023) has established itself in less than ten years as the reference email marketing platform for European small businesses and SMEs. Its positioning relies on an aggressive freemium model and broad functional scope: email, SMS, chat, light CRM, transactional.

Free features include:

- 300 emails sent per day with no contact limit.
- Drag-and-drop editor with over 70 responsive templates.
- Detailed statistics (open, click, unsubscribe, hard/soft bounce).
- GDPR compliance with European hosting.
- A/B test on subject and content.
- Transactional API for triggered emails (invoices, confirmations).

For structures wanting more advanced commercial steering, native integration with a [dedicated CRM software](/en/blog/best-crm-software/) remains preferable.

## Mailchimp and Mailjet: international and technical alternatives

**Mailchimp** remains in 2026 the most-used email marketing platform worldwide with over 12 million active accounts. Its strength lies in an exceptional integration ecosystem (Shopify, WooCommerce, Salesforce, HubSpot, over 300 native connectors) and a particularly polished user experience.

**Mailjet** stands out on the market by its technical orientation: co-founded by French developers, its API is one of the most appreciated by IT teams for transactional emails (application notifications, automated invoices, system alerts).

## ActiveCampaign, Klaviyo and marketing automation specialization

**ActiveCampaign** is the reference for advanced B2B marketing automation. Its strength lies in the depth of possible automated sequences: behavioral scoring, multi-level conditions, cross triggers (CRM, website, purchases, forms).

**Klaviyo** has established itself as the reference for e-commerce companies using Shopify, WooCommerce or BigCommerce. Its strength lies in native consideration of purchase behavior: abandoned cart, post-purchase sequence, reactivation of inactive customers.

For structures combining email marketing and complex marketing project management (campaigns coordinated across multiple channels), coupling with a [project management software](/en/blog/best-project-management-software/) facilitates coordination of content, design and acquisition teams.

## How to choose your email marketing software

Five criteria structure the decision:

**1. Monthly sending volume and contact base size**
Fewer than 5,000 emails per month and fewer than 1,000 contacts: free versions Brevo, Mailerlite or Mailjet suffice. Beyond, compare pricing to volume on paid formulas.

**2. Required automation level**
Occasional newsletters: Sarbacane, Mailerlite or basic Brevo. Advanced B2B marketing automation: ActiveCampaign, HubSpot or Brevo Plus. E-commerce with behavioral triggers: Klaviyo.

**3. Data localization and GDPR compliance**
For structures favoring European hosting: Brevo, Mailjet and Sarbacane store data in Europe. Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign and Klaviyo mainly use American datacenters.

**4. Integration with existing ecosystem**
HubSpot or Salesforce CRM: Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign integrate natively. WordPress CMS: almost all platforms offer a plugin.

**5. Support and accompaniment**
For French support with assisted onboarding: Brevo, Sarbacane or Mailjet. For mainly English support and self-onboarding: Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, Klaviyo.
