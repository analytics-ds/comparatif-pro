Project management certifications shape careers and validate skills with employers. PMP, PRINCE2, Scrum Master: choosing the right one requires understanding their positioning.

## Key takeaways

1. PMP remains the international benchmark for senior project managers.
2. PRINCE2 dominates in Europe and the British and French public sector.
3. Agile certifications (Scrum Master, PMI-ACP, SAFe) are growing in tech.
4. Average certification budget ranges from 1,500 to 4,500 EUR.

## Main certifications

PMP (Project Management Professional) delivered by PMI requires 35 hours of training and field experience. PRINCE2 Foundation and Practitioner structure projects in British environments. Scrum Master certifies Agile facilitators. PMI-ACP validates cross-functional Agile mastery.

## Certification comparison

| Certification | Total cost | Prerequisites | Recognition |
|---|---|---|---|
| PMP | 2,500 to 4,500 EUR | 3-5 years experience, 35h training | International |
| PRINCE2 Practitioner | 1,500 to 3,000 EUR | PRINCE2 Foundation | Europe, public sector |
| Scrum Master (PSM) | 200 to 1,500 EUR | None | Tech, digital |
| PMI-ACP | 2,000 to 3,500 EUR | 21h Agile training | Cross-functional Agile |

## FAQ

### Which project management certification to choose?

The choice depends on the industry and targeted role. PMP is the international benchmark, PRINCE2 dominates Europe and public sector, Scrum Master and PMI-ACP validate Agile skills.

### How much does a PMP certification cost?

Total cost of a PMP certification ranges from 2,500 to 4,500 EUR: 405 EUR for the exam, 139 EUR PMI membership, and 1,500 to 3,500 EUR of preparatory training.

### What is the market value of a PMP certification?

According to the 2026 PMI Earning Power Report, PMP certified professionals earn on average 16% more than non-certified peers in equivalent roles.
