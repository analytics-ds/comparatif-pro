## Overview of the global biotech and pharmaceutical job market

The global biopharmaceutical industry has expanded at 7 to 9 percent annually since 2020, driven by mRNA vaccine development, cell and gene therapies, and AI-enabled drug discovery. The industry employs over 4.9 million people worldwide according to EFPIA and IFPMA 2024 data, with revenue exceeding 1.6 trillion dollars globally. Major manufacturing and R&D hubs concentrate in Boston-Cambridge, San Francisco Bay Area, New Jersey, Basel, Dublin and Paris-Saclay.

The **best job boards in biotech and pharmaceutical** fall into three categories: sector-specific platforms (BioSpace, Pharmiweb), scientific career portals (Nature, Science Careers, FierceBiotech) and corporate careers pages of major players. Effective search combines three to five channels, as specialized sector platforms capture 65 percent of industry hiring according to the Observatoire Leem 2024.

### Structural tension on scientific recruitment

The 2025 ManpowerGroup Global Talent Shortage Survey confirms structural tension in the sector. 72 percent of biotech and pharma recruitment projects are considered difficult to fill. The scarcest profiles include regulatory affairs specialists, biostatisticians, bioprocessing engineers, gene therapy manufacturing experts and clinical data managers.

This tension translates into salary premiums. Median entry-level salary in the US pharmaceutical industry reached 78,000 dollars in 2024 according to US BLS, with PhD-level roles starting at 110,000 dollars. Europe shows similar dynamics with 42,500 euros in France (Leem 2024) and 65,000 euros in Switzerland at entry level.

## Dedicated platforms for biotech and pharmaceutical

Several platforms target biopharmaceutical profiles specifically, with qualified postings and specialized services.

### BioSpace and FierceBiotech, the US leaders

**BioSpace** is the leading US platform for the biopharmaceutical industry, with 10,000 active job postings at any time. The site publishes daily news and market intelligence on drug approvals, clinical trials and company movements. BioSpace is particularly strong on biotech startups funded by VCs in Boston and California.

**FierceBiotech Jobs** is the career portal linked to FiercePharma and FierceBiotech media. It lists around 6,000 active positions and benefits from editorial credibility with scientific readers. The platform covers executive, R&D, manufacturing, commercial and regulatory affairs roles.

### Pharmiweb and the European specialists

**Pharmiweb.jobs** is the largest pharmaceutical job board in Europe, with 8,000 active postings across 38 countries. The site publishes the annual Pharmiweb Salary Survey which constitutes a reference on compensation benchmarks across European markets. Major employers represented include GSK, Novartis, AstraZeneca, Roche, Sanofi and Pfizer.

**EFPIA Careers** (European Federation of Pharmaceutical Industries and Associations) provides a pan-European portal with dedicated entries per country federation (Leem in France, vfa in Germany, Farmindustria in Italy, Farmaindustria in Spain). **EMA Careers** (European Medicines Agency based in Amsterdam) lists regulatory and scientific positions at the EU regulator.

### Nature Biotechnology and Science Careers

**Nature Biotechnology Careers** (Springer Nature) focuses on the biotech niche with 2,500 active listings, primarily academic and industry R&D positions requiring PhD-level qualifications. The prestige of the Nature brand attracts top scientific talent and major employers including NIH-funded institutions.

**Science Careers** (AAAS) complements Nature with stronger US coverage. It lists around 3,000 biotech and pharma positions and publishes quarterly salary surveys on life sciences roles. These platforms are especially relevant for PhD holders, as detailed in our comparison of [job boards for PhDs and doctoral candidates](/en/blog/best-job-boards-phd-researchers/).

## Corporate careers pages and major employers

Major pharmaceutical companies operate sophisticated career portals. They concentrate a significant share of industry hiring, particularly for experienced roles.

- **Pfizer**: 80,000 employees worldwide, approximately 10,000 annual hires
- **Johnson & Johnson**: 140,000 employees, 15,000 annual hires across pharma and medtech
- **Novartis**: 110,000 employees, 8,000 annual hires
- **Merck & Co**: 71,000 employees, 6,500 annual hires

- **AstraZeneca**: 83,000 employees, 7,000 annual hires, strong growth post-COVID
- **GSK**: 67,000 employees, 6,000 annual hires
- **Sanofi**: 91,000 employees, 7,500 annual hires
- **Eli Lilly**: 43,000 employees, 5,000 annual hires, strong growth on GLP-1

### CDMOs, CROs and specialized staffing

Contract development and manufacturing organizations (CDMOs) and contract research organizations (CROs) account for a growing share of industry employment. Leaders include **IQVIA** (86,000 employees), **Icon plc** (40,000 employees), **Parexel** (21,000 employees), **Thermo Fisher Clinical Trials** (former PPD, 35,000 employees), **Syneos Health** (29,000 employees), **Charles River Laboratories** (22,000 employees) and **Catalent** (17,000 employees).

## How to optimize a biotech and pharma job search

The biopharmaceutical sector has specificities that require targeted preparation. Studies consistently show that candidates combining multiple channels achieve significantly better outcomes than those relying on generalists alone.

> "Recruitment in the pharmaceutical industry flows for 65 percent through specialized channels, with 42 percent via sector portals and 23 percent via corporate careers pages of laboratories."
> — Observatoire Leem des Métiers, 2024

### Effective application approach

1. Identify five to ten target companies by therapeutic area (oncology, neurology, rare diseases, vaccines, cell therapies) and function
2. Create optimized profiles on BioSpace, Pharmiweb.jobs, LinkedIn and Nature Biotechnology Careers with precise role keywords
3. Follow scientific and regulatory news through Endpoints News, STAT, FiercePharma, BioCentury and BioSpace Insights
4. Prepare a sector-compliant CV highlighting clinical trials experience, publications, regulatory submissions, and GxP compliance, applying documentation practices described in our [guide on how to create technical documentation](/en/blog/how-to-create-technical-documentation/)
5. Apply to graduate programs (Novartis NGLD, Pfizer Rotational, GSK Future Leaders), which recruit 1,500 graduates annually worldwide

## In-demand roles and valued certifications

The most sought-after functions in biotech and pharma in 2026 are:

- Clinical research associate (CRA): 8,000 open roles globally, salary 65,000 to 95,000 dollars entry
- Regulatory affairs manager: 5,000 roles, salary 90,000 to 140,000 dollars
- Biostatistician and data manager: 6,500 roles, salary 85,000 to 125,000 dollars
- Bioprocessing and GMP engineer: 4,500 roles, salary 80,000 to 110,000 dollars
- Gene therapy manufacturing scientist: 2,500 roles, salary premium up to 180,000 dollars
- Commercial medical science liaison: 3,000 roles, salary 130,000 to 170,000 dollars

Valued certifications include GCP (Good Clinical Practice), ICH guidelines mastery, ISO 13485 for medical devices, Six Sigma for manufacturing, and project management certifications like PMP. For project leadership roles, the certifications presented in our [comparison of project management certifications](/en/blog/project-management-training-certifications/) are particularly valued in international structures.
